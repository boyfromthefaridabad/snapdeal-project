Six DAX-driven analyses on a 220-product, 120-day e-commerce dataset — built to catch the products that look fine on any single metric, but are failing the business once price, rating, risk, and sales are read together. This site extends the same dataset and Power BI project completed during training; no new dataset was introduced.

The six analyses

01

Pricing vs Satisfaction Exceptions

Products priced above category average, rated below it, in the top-20% of risk score, and selling below the category median — a compounding failure table, not a single bad metric.

02

Promotion Effectiveness Trend

A 30-day non-promotional baseline discount, compared against live daily discount, to separate real promotional lift from prices quietly inflated before a "sale."

03

Discount vs Rating Causation

A regression scatter plus review-volume-controlled comparisons, testing whether discounts truly improve satisfaction — or just generate more reviews that regress ratings toward average.

04

Financially Risky Inventory KPI

Risk Score = Discount % × (1 − Rating/5) × Stock Value — rolled into a single traffic-light KPI card showing what share of inventory value is financially exposed.

05

Trust-Weighted Rating Index

Rating × log(1 + Review Count) × (1 − Return Rate) — surfacing why a "5-star, 4 reviews, 30% return rate" product is less trustworthy than a well-reviewed 4.3-star one.

06

Context-Aware Price Banding

Low/Medium/High bands set by the 30th/70th percentile of price — recalculated live as category, brand, or time slicers change, instead of one fixed threshold for the whole catalog.
